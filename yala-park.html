Unveiling a Kingdom of Biodiversity

Yala National Park is a tapestry woven with golden grasslands, ancient forests, and shimmering lagoons. This ecological haven shelters over 44 mammal species, 215 bird species, and countless reptiles and amphibians. Here, nature's pulse quickens, and every turn unveils a new wonder.

  • Witness elephants traversing the plains, their matriarchs leading the way with stoic wisdom.
  • Catch a glimpse of the elusive leopard, its stealthy movements cloaked in dappled sunlight.
  • Hear the haunting calls of langurs echoing through the canopy, a symphony of the unseen.
  • Marvel at the vibrant plumage of peacocks as they strut across the landscape, flamboyant royalty of the wild.

Meet the Faces of Yala

Sloth Bear (Melursus ursinus)

The honey-loving gentle giant, often spotted grazing on termite mounds.

Spotted Deer (Axis axis)

Graceful herbivores bounding through the grasslands, frequently seen in herds.

Asian Water Buffalo (Bubalus bubalis)

Powerful beasts wallowing in waterholes, providing a vital role in the ecosystem.

Mugger Crocodile (Crocodylus palustris)

Apex predators lurking in waterways, adding an element of thrilling caution to your exploration.
