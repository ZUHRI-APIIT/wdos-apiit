Explore the World of Sri Lankan Elephants

Learn about their complex social structures, playful interactions, and vital role in shaping ecosystems.

Did you know? Sri Lanka boasts the highest density of Asian elephants in the world, with over 5,000 individuals.

Embark on a Thrilling Quest for Sri Lankan Leopards

Discover their incredible hunting skills, solitary nature, and vital role in maintaining ecological balance.

Interesting fact: The Sri Lankan leopard is a unique subspecies, smaller than its African counterparts.

Symphony of Feathers: Sri Lanka's Bird Kingdom

Immerse yourself in the symphony of Sri Lanka's bird kingdom, where over 450 species paint the skies with their colors and fill the air with their songs.

Fun fact: Sri Lanka is a birder's paradise, boasting several endemic species found nowhere else on Earth.

Interesting Facts

  • raven
    Sri Lanka is home to 16 endemic mammals, including the sloth bear, the toque macaque, and the slender loris.
  • sailing
    Over 100 reptile and amphibian species thrive in diverse habitats, including the colorful Sri Lankan green pit viper and the rare hump-nosed lizard.
  • beach_access
    Five marine turtle species nest on the island's sandy beaches, making it a crucial nesting ground for these endangered creatures.

Wildife Activites.

  • 01
    Witness the annual elephant gathering at Minneriya National Park, one of the largest gatherings of land mammals in the world.
  • 02
    Go whale watching off the coast of Mirissa, where you might encounter blue whales, the largest animals on Earth.
  • 03
    Hike through Sinharaja Forest, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a treasure trove of endemic flora and fauna.
